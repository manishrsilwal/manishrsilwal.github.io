mySecondTeacher

An ed-tech platform built for students, teachers, parents, and school admins. Four audiences, one product, across desktop, tablet, and mobile.

Role Senior Product Designer
Company Innovate Tech
Timeline 2020 — 2025
Type Product Design — Desktop, Tablet & Mobile

The Problem

mySecondTeacher needed to serve four very different groups of people: students watching lessons, teachers uploading content, parents checking progress, and admins running the show. Each group has its own priorities, its own mental model, and its own level of comfort with technology. And they all need to use the same product.

What I Did

I designed role-based dashboards that share a single design system but surface completely different content depending on who’s logged in. A teacher’s first screen looks nothing like a student’s, and that’s intentional.

The hardest calls were about what to leave out. Students don’t need admin controls. Parents don’t need content upload tools. Every screen had to earn its place by answering one question: does this person need this right now?

I also pushed back on a number of feature requests that would have added clutter to the student experience. For students, less is almost always better.

The Result

The platform shipped across web, mobile, and tablet. Teachers reported that content management felt faster after the redesign, and the role-based approach meant each user group got an experience that actually felt like it was built for them.
